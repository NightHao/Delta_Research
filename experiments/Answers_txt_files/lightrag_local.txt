Question: What is the system architecture of Vehicle-to-Grid?

Answer: ## System Architecture of Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G)

The system architecture of Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) communication is organized along architectural lines that emphasize the separation of the system into distinct layers. This architecture is designed to facilitate efficient communication between electric vehicles (EVs) and the electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE) during charging sessions.

### Communication Layers Overview

1. **Physical Layer**: This layer is responsible for the actual transmission of data over the communication medium. It includes the hardware components that connect the EV to the EVSE.

2. **Data Link Layer**: This layer manages the data exchange between the EV and the EVSE, ensuring that the communication is reliable and error-free. It includes the MAC (Media Access Control) sublayer, which handles the protocol for accessing the physical medium.

3. **Higher Layers (Network to Application)**: The architecture also encompasses layers that handle network protocols, transport, session management, presentation, and application functionalities. These layers are defined in the broader context of the ISO 15118 standard, which specifies the requirements for V2G communication.

### High-Level Communication (HLC)

The V2G architecture incorporates High-Level Communication (HLC) in addition to basic signaling. HLC enables bidirectional communication, allowing for more complex interactions between the EV and the grid. The data exchange during a V2G session is structured into three main periods:

- **Data Link Setup**: Establishing the communication link between the EV and the EVSE.
- **V2G Setup**: Initializing the vehicle-to-grid communication, including authorization and configuration.
- **V2G Charging Loop**: The ongoing process of energy exchange and management during the charging session.

### Conclusion

This layered architecture ensures that V2G communication is robust, efficient, and capable of supporting the various functionalities required for effective energy management and grid stabilization.
